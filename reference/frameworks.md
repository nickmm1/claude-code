## The Five Forces Framework (Trademark Pending)

Nick's core coaching methodology. Five forces that drive salon business success, taught in rotating 5-week cycles (10 cycles per year). Each force has specific named sub-systems and tools.

**Force 1: Vision and Model**
- Clarity on goals, direction, life design
- The Personal Economy: building multiple revenue streams beyond the chair (services, retail, e-commerce, education, new locations)
- The Vision Pyramid: Life Vision, Business Vision, Service Vision
- The salon's Ikigai: finding the intersection of passion, skill, market need, and profitability
- The Domination Statement: clear, specific positioning that makes the salon THE obvious choice in their market
- Taught on coaching calls. Vision is qualitative and personal. No automated report for this force. Nick coaches it directly.

**Force 2: Demand and Marketing**
- Visibility, SEO, social media, client attraction, content strategy
- The 7 Demand Channels: Search, Social, Reputation, Paid, Direct, Community, Database
- The Search Games System: how to dominate local search results
- Answer Engine Optimization (AEO): optimizing for AI search (ChatGPT, Google AI Overviews, voice search)
- Local SEO domination strategy
- Content strategy and content calendar creation
- **Automated Report: Visibility Blueprint (onboarding) and Monthly Visibility Report (ongoing)**
- Data sources: Apify scraping (Instagram, Facebook, website, GBP, competitors) + Search Atlas (Inner Circle only)

**Force 3: Client Journey and Sales**
- Conversions, rebooking, upselling, client experience
- The Raving Fan System: consultations that convert, retail that serves, rebooking on autopilot
- The Client Journey: 7-touchpoint system for turning strangers into lifetime clients
- The C.A.R.E. Consultation Framework: structured consultations that build trust and drive sales
- Permission-based selling: earning the right to recommend, not pushing products
- **Automated Report: NPS Client Experience Report (monthly)**
- Data sources: Client feedback form submissions via QR code/unique URL per salon

**Force 4: Profit and Protection**
- Pricing, margins, financial mastery, wealth building
- Profit First methodology: 5 bank accounts, pay yourself before anything else, every month
- Parts + Labor pricing: pricing services based on actual cost of delivery plus desired margin
- Real Revenue: the actual profit after every expense, not what the POS says
- Commission structure optimization
- Chair rent optimization
- **Automated Report: Profit First Blueprint (onboarding) and Monthly Profit Report (ongoing)**
- Data sources: AI Accountant (hosted on Manus)

**Force 5: Leadership and Systems**
- Team building, culture, delegation, operations
- The Replacement Ladder: systematically replacing yourself in the business by transferring tasks and roles
- The Camcorder Method: seeing your business the way clients and team actually experience it
- Extreme Ownership: building a culture of accountability, not excuses
- The Five Dysfunctions of a Salon Team: diagnosing and fixing team dysfunction
- The Growth Conversation Framework: structured conversations for developing team members
- One-on-ones: how to run effective individual meetings with team members
- Team meetings: how to conduct meetings that move the needle
- Taught on coaching calls. Leadership is qualitative. No standalone automated report, but the data from Forces 2, 3, and 4 informs the leadership coaching conversation.

## The SPARC Teaching Method

Every lesson, workshop, coaching call outline, and teaching session must follow this framework.

- **S: State Shift.** Clear the mental clutter first. Open with an Uncomfortable Question, a Pattern Interrupt Story, or a Physical Reset. Get them out of their head and into the room.
- **P: Paradigm Shift.** Show them that the way they see the problem IS the problem. Use The Mirror (show them their own behavior), a Contrast Frame (show them two realities), or a Connection Question (make them feel the gap).
- **A: Architect the Solution.** Build the framework using See, Do, Repeat. Use a Teach-Test Loop. Maximum 4 ideas per lesson. More than 4 and retention drops. Keep it focused.
- **R: Rapid Success.** Create immediate action. Use Slow Burn (small daily actions), Immersion (intensive implementation), or Teach to Learn (have them explain it to someone else).
- **C: Commit to the Change.** Lock the learning in. Use a Declaration (public commitment on the call), Teach-Back (have them explain it back), Spaced Follow-Up (revisit next week), or Community Share (post their commitment in the group).

**Rule: All teaching content must follow SPARC. Every lesson opens with a State Shift, reframes with a Paradigm Shift, teaches with Architect the Solution (max 4 ideas, See-Do-Repeat), creates action with Rapid Success, and locks in with Commit to the Change.**

## The Five-Week Cycle Depth Progression

Each time a force cycles back (every 5 weeks), the depth increases:

- Cycle 1-2: Awareness. "Here's the problem you did not know you had."
- Cycle 3-4: Diagnosis. "Here's how bad it is in your salon."
- Cycle 5-6: Strategy. "Here's the framework to fix it."
- Cycle 7-8: Implementation. "Build the solution live."
- Cycle 9-10: Optimization. "Refine and improve what you built."
